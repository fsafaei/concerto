## Context
v0.2 §6.2 default position is exponential CBF in Phase 2. ADR resolves the formulation choice and its assumption envelope, citing the Tier-1 Huriot & Sibai 2025 read (note 42, mislabelled "Singh" in schedule.csv) and the Tier-2 safety cluster (notes 37, 44, 45). Foundational anchors for the formulation are Ames et al. 2017 (canonical CBF-QP safety filter, IEEE TAC) and Ames et al. 2019 (ECC tutorial); the conformal layer rests on Shafer & Vovk 2008 and Angelopoulos & Bates 2023. The core tension: exponential CBF (Option A) is mature and certifiable but requires known partner dynamics, while our black-box partner setting invalidates that assumption at deployment.

## Considered alternatives

| # | Option | Source / advocate | Pros | Cons |
|---|--------|-------------------|------|------|
| A | Exponential CBF | [Ames et al. 2017 IEEE TAC; Tier-2 #37 W-A-E; Tier-2 #38 Cheng] | Mature; tractable; certifiable | Conservative; requires known dynamics; high-relative-degree systems need work |
| B | High-order CBF | [Tier-3 #43 Kim 2026] | Handles higher-DoF coupling | Newer; less literature on manipulation |
| C | MPC safety filter | [external] | Predictive horizon useful for Tier 3 | Computational cost; harder to certify |
| D | Conformal CBF (Huriot & Sibai-style) | [Tier-1 #42 Huriot & Sibai] | Adapts restrictiveness to observed prediction error; black-box partner OK; distribution-free guarantee | Partner predictor must exist; requires online recalibration; average-loss (not per-step) guarantee |

## Decision
Exponential CBF (Option A) in Phase 2 with a conformal-adaptation overlay from Huriot & Sibai 2025 (Option D) to handle the black-box partner. The within-robot manipulation-grade filter uses the OSCBF structure from Morton & Pavone 2025 (note 44). High-order CBF is a Phase-3 research extension; MPC is reserved for Tier-3 external use only.

## Rationale
Ames, Xu, Grizzle, and Tabuada (2017) establish the canonical CBF-QP safety filter — min ‖u−û‖² subject to a forward-invariant CBF constraint — that the project's entire safety stack inherits. Ames et al. (2019, ECC) consolidates the theory; Wang, Ames, and Egerstedt (2017) (notes/tier2/37_wang_ames_egerstedt_2017.md) extend it to decentralized multi-robot collision avoidance with the proportional constraint-budget split (αi/(αi+αj) by acceleration limit) that directly handles heterogeneous embodiments in §6.2; this pattern is retained as the structural backbone because it wraps any nominal controller without accessing its internals, which is precisely the property needed when the partner policy is black-box. Garg et al. 2024 (notes/tier2/45_lindemann_safety_survey.md) confirm via Table 1 that no existing method simultaneously achieves formal safety guarantees, dynamics-free operation, and distributed deployment — establishing that Option A alone is insufficient and that the dynamics-free axis must be addressed by the conformal extension. Huriot & Sibai 2025 (notes/tier1/42_singh_2024.md) close this gap: their conformal slack variable λ (eq. 5) added additively to each CBF constraint, updated by λ_{k+1} = λ_k + η(ε − l_k), provides a distribution-free ε + o(1) long-term risk bound (Theorem 3) that holds even under adversarial partner predictions, making Option D the necessary extension of Option A rather than a competing alternative. The conformal slack variable inherits the distribution-free coverage guarantees of conformal prediction (Shafer & Vovk 2008; Angelopoulos & Bates 2023); Huriot & Sibai 2025 specialise it to a CBF online-update setting, producing the ε + o(1) long-term risk bound. Morton & Pavone 2025 (notes/tier2/44_morton_2025.md) supply the within-robot manipulator-grade filter: the OSCBF two-level QP (‖Wⱼ(q̇−q̇_nom)‖² + ‖Wₒ(ν−ν_nom)‖² with slack relaxation for conflicting constraints) operates at kilohertz rates on a 7-DOF arm and directly addresses the CBF-QP infeasibility problem flagged in Garg et al. §7.3. Options B and C are deferred because HO-CBF's manipulation literature is immature (Tier-3, no done note) and MPC's certification cost outweighs its predictive benefit at Phase-2 scope.

The conformal update rule λ_{k+1} = λ_k + η(ε − l_k) (Huriot & Sibai 2025 §IV.A; Theorem 3) is driven by the **prediction-gap loss** l_k = max(0, predicted_h − actual_h), evaluated by the partner-trajectory predictor against the realised partner state — not by the per-step constraint-violation signal max(0, −h_ij) that the exp CBF-QP backbone produces. Theorem 3's distribution-free ε + o(1) average-loss bound is stated against the prediction gap; driving the update from −h would conflate predictor error with per-step CBF feasibility and break the cited guarantee. The implementation reflects this separation: `concerto.safety.cbf_qp.ExpCBFQP` populates `FilterInfo["constraint_violation"]` only, and `concerto.safety.conformal.update_lambda_from_predictor` (with the M3 constant-velocity predictor stub) computes and applies the prediction-gap loss, populating `FilterInfo["prediction_gap_loss"]`. The ADR-014 three-table report carries the two signals in distinct cells so per-step violations and predictor error are visible side-by-side.

## Mode and per-agent control model

The filter's variable layout and partner role are made explicit by a `SafetyMode` enum on `concerto.safety.cbf_qp.ExpCBFQP` and a per-uid `AgentControlModel` Protocol on `concerto.safety.api`. The full taxonomy lives in [spike_004A_safety_mode.md](spike_004A_safety_mode.md); the summary here is the contract this ADR commits to.

- **`SafetyMode.EGO_ONLY` — deployment / ad-hoc / black-box-partner default.** The QP decides only the ego agent's action; the partner's motion enters as a predicted disturbance on the constraint right-hand side. This is the mode `concerto.safety.cbf_qp.ExpCBFQP` ships in by default and the mode every ADR-009 partner-zoo evaluation must use.
- **`SafetyMode.CENTRALIZED` — oracle / ablation only.** The QP decides every uid's action; per-uid slot widths come from `control_models[uid].action_dim` with no "first uid's shape" fallback. Used as the upper-bound baseline in the ADR-014 three-table report; never used at evaluation against ad-hoc partners.
- **`SafetyMode.SHARED_CONTROL` — lab-only baseline.** Same shape as `CENTRALIZED` with an additional `partner_action_bound` that restricts the partner's adjustment magnitude. Measures how much of the centralised headroom comes from co-control budget vs from partner co-operation.

Mode is constructor-scoped, not per-call: `SafetyState.lambda_` is sized per pair, and the pair set differs between `EGO_ONLY` (one row per partner) and `CENTRALIZED` (full pairwise table). Flipping mode mid-episode would silently invalidate the conformal state; a mode change requires constructing a new filter with a fresh `SafetyState`. This is the first of two design judgment calls recorded for the §Risks #4 mitigation below.

The `AgentControlModel` Protocol mediates between each agent's action space and the shared Cartesian / safety space. The CBF row is built in Cartesian acceleration variables (`n_hat^T (a_i - a_j)`) and projected to action-space coefficients via each uid's Jacobian. For a `DoubleIntegratorControlModel` (action == Cartesian acceleration) the Jacobian is the identity and the row coefficients are byte-for-byte identical to the pre-refactor backbone; this is the migration shim every Phase-0 homogeneous test uses. For a `JacobianControlModel` (Stage-1 AS spike deliverable) the Jacobian is supplied via a callable; the placeholder raises `NotImplementedError` so 7-DOF uids fail loudly rather than silently corrupting an action slot — the same loud-fail discipline `JacobianEmergencyController` already established at the fallback boundary.

The OSCBF inner filter is *not* parameterised by `AgentControlModel`: OSCBF operates in joint space and consumes its own Jacobian (Morton & Pavone 2025 §IV). The control-model interface lives at the *inter-agent* boundary; OSCBF lives at the *per-arm* boundary. This is the second design judgment call: the `tests/property/test_oscbf.py` invariants are explicitly preserved through the refactor without being routed through `DoubleIntegratorControlModel`, because the routing would be a no-op (the migration plan in [spike_004A §Migration plan](spike_004A_safety_mode.md#-migration-plan) records this).

## Consequences
- Project scope: B0 safety layer is a two-tier filter — outer tier: exp CBF-QP with conformal λ adaptation handling inter-robot collision constraints where the partner trajectory is predicted by a black-box predictor; inner tier: OSCBF QP handling within-robot task-consistency constraints at actuator command level. HO-CBF and MPC are explicitly out of Phase-2 scope.
- v0.2 plan sections affected: §6 safety architecture (conformal constraint eq. 5, update rule, ε < 0 for manipulation); §6.2 manipulator-grade filter (OSCBF two-level QP, actuator-command-level interception per Table I forewarning, per-pair budget split αi/(αi+αj)); §13 baseline design must include B_safety = pred/noLearn (λ=η=0, black-box predictor) as the safety-ablation baseline.
- Other ADRs: depends on ADR-006 (partner-policy assumption set must bound prediction error E_v and E_d to satisfy Huriot & Sibai Assumption 2); depends on ADR-007 (heterogeneity axes determine per-agent acceleration limits αi for budget split in §6.2). **Precondition for ADR-007 rev 3 Stage 3 SA spike**: the exp CBF + conformal overlay + OSCBF stack defined here must be functional (running on the Stage-1-validated rig with at least one contact-rich task) before Stage 3 SA begins; the SA spike measures the ≥20pp gap by varying per-vendor force-limit / SIL-PL pairs against this filter, so the filter cannot itself be the variable. **If ADR-007 Open Q #4 resolves to "decompose"** (safety axis splits into ISO/TS 15066-style force-limit compliance vs ISO 10218-2:2025-style functional-safety SIL/PL ratings), this ADR's filter must be extended with a per-vendor force-limit / SIL-PL handler — currently the formulation assumes a single global force threshold per pair, and ISO/TS 15066's absorption into ISO 10218-2:2025 makes per-vendor compliance variance a binding-standard concern; flagged as Open question #5 below.
- Compute / time / hiring: QP solve at kilohertz feasible on GPU; λ update is O(1) per control step; no additional hiring required in Phase 2 beyond safety engineer already planned for §6.

## Risks and mitigations
- [Theorem 3 is an average-loss guarantee, not per-step collision avoidance] In contact-rich manipulation a single step of constraint violation can cause irreversible physical damage; the conformal bound does not prevent such a spike. → Mitigation: set ε < 0 (conservative regime, per note 42 §Implications) to bias λ toward tighter constraints during contact phases; add the Wang et al. hybrid braking fallback (eq. 17 in note 37) as a hard safety backstop that bypasses the conformal QP when a braking-distance threshold is breached. The per-step backstop is embodiment-routed via the `EmergencyController` Protocol in `concerto.safety.emergency`: `maybe_brake` aggregates the per-pair Cartesian push-apart unit vectors per uid (preserving superposition when one uid is in multiple simultaneous dangerous pairs) and then dispatches each uid's aggregate to a per-uid controller that owns the Cartesian-to-control mapping. The default `CartesianAccelEmergencyController` sum-and-saturates in Cartesian space and is correct only for double-integrator agents whose control dimension equals the Cartesian dimension (the Wang-Ames-Egerstedt 2017 §V toy crossing and the Phase-0 smoke tests). The Jacobian-aware override for 7-DOF manipulators is a Stage-1 deliverable flagged in ADR-007 §"Stage 1 — Foundation axes" AS spike scope (7-DOF arm vs 2-DOF differential-drive base on a shared pick-place task); the placeholder `JacobianEmergencyController` raises `NotImplementedError` so 7-DOF uids fail loudly at the fallback boundary rather than silently writing a Cartesian-shaped vector into a joint-torque slot.
- [λ stationarity assumption breaks on partner swap] The conformal update rule assumes prediction errors are stationary; a mid-episode partner replacement (§3.5 partner zoo evaluation) invalidates this, potentially leaving λ calibrated to the wrong partner and creating an unsafe transient. → Mitigation: reinitialize λ to λ_safe (the value guaranteeing QP feasibility under worst-case bounded prediction error) on any partner-identity change detected by the capability-inference module (§3.3); treat the first N_warmup steps after swap as a high-caution window with ε_warmup < ε.
- [OSCBF known-dynamics assumption at new embodiments] Morton & Pavone 2025 require the full dynamics model M(q), c(q,q̇), g(q); adding a new arm embodiment requires system identification that introduces parametric uncertainty. → Mitigation: bound identification error as an additive disturbance in the CBF constraint slack; set the minimum slack margin ≥ worst-case identification error norm, consistent with the conformal-extension framing in note 42 and the per-step safety argument.
- [Pre-refactor QP layout assumed homogeneous action_dim and co-controllable partner] The pre-refactor `concerto.safety.cbf_qp.ExpCBFQP` derived `n_u_per_agent` from the first uid's action shape and returned safe actions for every uid in a dict — a homogeneous, centralised solve. Reviewer P0-2 flagged this as inconsistent with the AS axis of ADR-007 (7-DOF arm vs 2-DOF base requires per-uid `action_dim`); reviewer P0-3 flagged the centralised solve as inconsistent with the black-box-partner contract of ADR-006 (deployment must not co-control the partner). → Mitigation: the heterogeneity-aware refactor specified in [spike_004A_safety_mode.md](spike_004A_safety_mode.md) and summarised in §"Mode and per-agent control model" above. `SafetyMode.EGO_ONLY` is the deployment default and routes the partner through the constraint RHS as a predicted disturbance; per-uid `AgentControlModel` carries the action ↔ Cartesian map so heterogeneous embodiments build correctly-shaped rows. The placeholder `JacobianControlModel` raises `NotImplementedError` until the Stage-1 AS spike supplies a kinematic model — the same loud-fail discipline that protects the fallback path. Two design judgment calls recorded: (1) mode is constructor-scoped, so flipping it mid-episode cannot silently invalidate the conformal slack vector's per-pair sizing; (2) `tests/property/test_oscbf.py` is intentionally not routed through `DoubleIntegratorControlModel` because OSCBF is per-arm joint-space and already Jacobian-parameterised — the routing would be a no-op and the spec instruction does not apply.

## Reversibility
Type-2 (two-way door). The CBF formulation (order, function family) is encapsulated in the QP constraint-generation module; switching from exp CBF to HO-CBF in Phase 3 requires refactoring that module only. The conformal adaptation layer and the OSCBF QP wrapper interface are order-agnostic. No capital expenditure, irreversible infrastructure, or external commitment is gated on the Phase-2 formulation choice.

## Validation criteria
By Phase-2 end: (1) B0 with CBF-QP + conformal safety filter achieves <2% inter-robot collision rate across ≥500 evaluation episodes under standard conditions and <5% under 200ms comm latency + 30% packet drop; (2) conformal λ update reduces collision rate by ≥20% relative to the pred/noLearn ablation baseline (λ=η=0, matching Huriot & Sibai Table I structure); (3) OSCBF QP solve time <1ms per control step on Phase-2 evaluation hardware.
