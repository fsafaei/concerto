# ADR-004: Safety filter formulation — exp CBF, HO-CBF, MPC, learned

**Status.** Proposed (dependency update 2026-05-08 in light of ADR-007 rev 3 staged rollout)
**Authors.** Farhad Safaei
**Reviewers.** _to fill_
**Tags.** v0.2 §6.2 + §13, SAFE

## Context
v0.2 §6.2 default position is exponential CBF in Phase 2. ADR resolves the formulation choice and its assumption envelope, citing the Tier-1 Huriot & Sibai 2025 read (note 42, mislabelled "Singh" in schedule.csv) and the Tier-2 safety cluster (notes 37, 44, 45). Foundational anchors for the formulation are Ames et al. 2017 (canonical CBF-QP safety filter, IEEE TAC) and Ames et al. 2019 (ECC tutorial); the conformal layer rests on Shafer & Vovk 2008 and Angelopoulos & Bates 2023. The core tension: exponential CBF (Option A) is mature and certifiable but requires known partner dynamics, while our black-box partner setting invalidates that assumption at deployment.

## Decision
Exponential CBF (Option A) in Phase 2 with a conformal-adaptation overlay from Huriot & Sibai 2025 (Option D) to handle the black-box partner. The within-robot manipulation-grade filter uses the OSCBF structure from Morton & Pavone 2025 (note 44). High-order CBF is a Phase-3 research extension; MPC is reserved for Tier-3 external use only.

## Rationale
Ames, Xu, Grizzle, and Tabuada (2017) establish the canonical CBF-QP safety filter — min ‖u−û‖² subject to a forward-invariant CBF constraint — that the project's entire safety stack inherits. Ames et al. (2019, ECC) consolidates the theory; Wang, Ames, and Egerstedt (2017) (notes/tier2/37_wang_ames_egerstedt_2017.md) extend it to decentralized multi-robot collision avoidance with the proportional constraint-budget split (αi/(αi+αj) by acceleration limit) that directly handles heterogeneous embodiments in §6.2; this pattern is retained as the structural backbone because it wraps any nominal controller without accessing its internals, which is precisely the property needed when the partner policy is black-box. Garg et al. 2024 (notes/tier2/45_lindemann_safety_survey.md) confirm via Table 1 that no existing method simultaneously achieves formal safety guarantees, dynamics-free operation, and distributed deployment — establishing that Option A alone is insufficient and that the dynamics-free axis must be addressed by the conformal extension. Huriot & Sibai 2025 (notes/tier1/42_singh_2024.md) close this gap: their conformal slack variable λ (eq. 5) added additively to each CBF constraint, updated by λ_{k+1} = λ_k + η(ε − l_k), provides a distribution-free ε + o(1) long-term risk bound (Theorem 3) that holds even under adversarial partner predictions, making Option D the necessary extension of Option A rather than a competing alternative. The conformal slack variable inherits the distribution-free coverage guarantees of conformal prediction (Shafer & Vovk 2008; Angelopoulos & Bates 2023); Huriot & Sibai 2025 specialise it to a CBF online-update setting, producing the ε + o(1) long-term risk bound. Morton & Pavone 2025 (notes/tier2/44_morton_2025.md) supply the within-robot manipulator-grade filter: the OSCBF two-level QP (‖Wⱼ(q̇−q̇_nom)‖² + ‖Wₒ(ν−ν_nom)‖² with slack relaxation for conflicting constraints) operates at kilohertz rates on a 7-DOF arm and directly addresses the CBF-QP infeasibility problem flagged in Garg et al. §7.3. Options B and C are deferred because HO-CBF's manipulation literature is immature (Tier-3, no done note) and MPC's certification cost outweighs its predictive benefit at Phase-2 scope.

## Consequences
- Project scope: B0 safety layer is a two-tier filter — outer tier: exp CBF-QP with conformal λ adaptation handling inter-robot collision constraints where the partner trajectory is predicted by a black-box predictor; inner tier: OSCBF QP handling within-robot task-consistency constraints at actuator command level. HO-CBF and MPC are explicitly out of Phase-2 scope.
- v0.2 plan sections affected: §6 safety architecture (conformal constraint eq. 5, update rule, ε < 0 for manipulation); §6.2 manipulator-grade filter (OSCBF two-level QP, actuator-command-level interception per Table I forewarning, per-pair budget split αi/(αi+αj)); §13 baseline design must include B_safety = pred/noLearn (λ=η=0, black-box predictor) as the safety-ablation baseline.
- Other ADRs: depends on ADR-006 (partner-policy assumption set must bound prediction error E_v and E_d to satisfy Huriot & Sibai Assumption 2); depends on ADR-007 (heterogeneity axes determine per-agent acceleration limits αi for budget split in §6.2). **Precondition for ADR-007 rev 3 Stage 3 SA spike**: the exp CBF + conformal overlay + OSCBF stack defined here must be functional (running on the Stage-1-validated rig with at least one contact-rich task) before Stage 3 SA begins; the SA spike measures the ≥20pp gap by varying per-vendor force-limit / SIL-PL pairs against this filter, so the filter cannot itself be the variable. **If ADR-007 Open Q #4 resolves to "decompose"** (safety axis splits into ISO/TS 15066-style force-limit compliance vs ISO 10218-2:2025-style functional-safety SIL/PL ratings), this ADR's filter must be extended with a per-vendor force-limit / SIL-PL handler — currently the formulation assumes a single global force threshold per pair, and ISO/TS 15066's absorption into ISO 10218-2:2025 makes per-vendor compliance variance a binding-standard concern; flagged as Open question #5 below.
- Compute / time / hiring: QP solve at kilohertz feasible on GPU; λ update is O(1) per control step; no additional hiring required in Phase 2 beyond safety engineer already planned for §6.

## Risks and mitigations
- [Theorem 3 is an average-loss guarantee, not per-step collision avoidance] In contact-rich manipulation a single step of constraint violation can cause irreversible physical damage; the conformal bound does not prevent such a spike. → Mitigation: set ε < 0 (conservative regime, per note 42 §Implications) to bias λ toward tighter constraints during contact phases; add the Wang et al. hybrid braking fallback (eq. 17 in note 37) as a hard safety backstop that bypasses the conformal QP when a braking-distance threshold is breached.
- [λ stationarity assumption breaks on partner swap] The conformal update rule assumes prediction errors are stationary; a mid-episode partner replacement (§3.5 partner zoo evaluation) invalidates this, potentially leaving λ calibrated to the wrong partner and creating an unsafe transient. → Mitigation: reinitialize λ to λ_safe (the value guaranteeing QP feasibility under worst-case bounded prediction error) on any partner-identity change detected by the capability-inference module (§3.3); treat the first N_warmup steps after swap as a high-caution window with ε_warmup < ε.
- [OSCBF known-dynamics assumption at new embodiments] Morton & Pavone 2025 require the full dynamics model M(q), c(q,q̇), g(q); adding a new arm embodiment requires system identification that introduces parametric uncertainty. → Mitigation: bound identification error as an additive disturbance in the CBF constraint slack; set the minimum slack margin ≥ worst-case identification error norm, consistent with the conformal-extension framing in note 42 and the per-step safety argument.

## Reversibility
Type-2 (two-way door). The CBF formulation (order, function family) is encapsulated in the QP constraint-generation module; switching from exp CBF to HO-CBF in Phase 3 requires refactoring that module only. The conformal adaptation layer and the OSCBF QP wrapper interface are order-agnostic. No capital expenditure, irreversible infrastructure, or external commitment is gated on the Phase-2 formulation choice.

## Validation criteria
By Phase-2 end: (1) B0 with CBF-QP + conformal safety filter achieves <2% inter-robot collision rate across ≥500 evaluation episodes under standard conditions and <5% under 200ms comm latency + 30% packet drop; (2) conformal λ update reduces collision rate by ≥20% relative to the pred/noLearn ablation baseline (λ=η=0, matching Huriot & Sibai Table I structure); (3) OSCBF QP solve time <1ms per control step on Phase-2 evaluation hardware.
