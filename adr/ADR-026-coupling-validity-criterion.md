# ADR-026: Coupling-validity criterion for heterogeneity axes (and reinterpretation of the Stage-1 AS verdict)

Number 026 is provisional: the first free integer (017 Accepted; 018–020 reserved for the proposed open-core / realism / certification governance ADRs; 021–025 for the proposed RSA-ADR cluster per the reference-system-architecture §18.2), so the 018–025 gap is intentional — confirm or fold at the one-shot ADR-INDEX reconciliation. **Authors.** Farhad Safaei
**Reviewers.** The project's structured internal review record (2026-06-15) — methodology/statistics, HMARL/robotics-domain, and hostile-reader seats; a documented internal review protocol, not an external assessment.
**Tags.** v0.2 §3.2, §3.4; heterogeneity-axis validation; supersedes-in-part ADR-007 §Validation criteria + §Stage-1 framing.

## Context

ADR-007 §Validation criteria gates each heterogeneity axis on a single magnitude rule: a ≥20 pp homogeneous-vs-heterogeneous success-rate gap (proposed to become a two-part rule under the ADR-008 gate-form amendment). The Stage-1 Action-Space (AS) axis was measured at gate scale on 2026-06-11 and returned a **sign-reversed** result — AS-homogeneous 1/100 vs AS-heterogeneous 12/100 pooled — recorded as an immutable verdict (`spikes/results/stage1-AS-2026-06-11/`, exit 5). A subsequent exploratory homo-static probe (partner fully frozen) returned 0/90, excluding partner *motion* as the explanation.

A 2026-06-15 construct-validity review established, from the environment source rather than from the outcome, that the Stage-1 pick-place task is **ego-solvable**: the success predicate (`chamber.envs.stage1_pickplace.Stage1PickPlaceEnv.evaluate`) reads only the ego agent, and the scripted partner (`chamber.partners.heuristic`) never manipulates the object (corroborated in `spikes/results/stage1-failure-investigation/2026-06-11-gate-verdict/AS_HOMO_OPERATIONALIZATION_2026-06-11.md`). The manipulated variable — whether the partner shares the ego's embodiment — could therefore enter the outcome only as incidental physical disturbance whose magnitude was set by untuned rig defaults, not through any cooperation the task demands. The recorded gap is consequently uninterpretable as evidence about heterogeneity-on-cooperation.

This decision is forced now because (i) the immutable verdict needs a recorded, principled interpretation before any downstream narrative leans on it; (ii) the next planned spend (the Observation-Modality axis) would, under the same analysis, stand up another axis whose two conditions hold the robot pair identical, measuring the ego's own sensor richness rather than cooperation; and (iii) the project lacked, before this review, any stated requirement that a heterogeneity manipulation be *coupled to the outcome through cooperation*. That requirement is **new** — it does not appear in ADR-007 §Validation criteria as locked before the gate fired — and is introduced here explicitly as a new requirement, not retrofitted as if it had always been present.

## Decision

Adopt **Option C**, with the conditions the review imposed.

**1. The coupling-validity criterion (binding validation requirement).** A heterogeneity axis is informative about cooperation only if the manipulated heterogeneity is coupled to the task outcome through the cooperation the task demands. Coupling-validity is a property of the **task × axis combination**, not of an axis in the abstract: an axis that is non-coupling-valid on an ego-solvable task (AS, OM, CR as currently operationalized) may be coupling-valid on a task that is infeasible for one robot. No heterogeneity axis is promoted to Validated on a task for which the coupling-validity criterion is not demonstrated.

**2. The criterion is falsifiable, not an escape hatch.** Adoption is conditional on an operational test that distinguishes "axis not coupling-valid" from "axis coupling-valid but no effect exists": every coupling-valid axis measurement must pre-register a **coupling positive-control** — a matched-pair-vs-single-robot contrast demonstrating the task is two-robot-infeasible (single-robot success ≈ 0) and that the coupling constraints actually bind on successful matched episodes — and a pre-committed rule that "positive control passes AND the heterogeneity effect is null (CI excludes Δ_min)" is reported as a **thesis-disconfirming null**, never re-described as a coupling failure.

**3. Reinterpretation of the recorded AS verdict (not an edit).** The `stage1-AS-2026-06-11` archive is immutable and stays untouched (I8). It is reinterpreted here as **uninterpretable for the cooperation thesis** — in either direction — for two independent reasons: (i) construct-invalidity (the task is ego-solvable; the partner does not participate); and (ii) a pre-registration-fidelity gap (the registered homogeneous baseline, shared-parameter MAPPO, was never built; an identical ego-AHT learner ran against a scripted partner in both conditions). The recorded sign-reversal must **not** be presented as evidence that "homogeneous conditions are harder": the homo-static probe and the surviving readings leave the arm-presence reading and the selection-pressure reading (a shaping coefficient selected on heterogeneous cells only) unseparated, and the diagnostic that would separate them was deliberately not run. The recorded statistical artifact — a tight interval alongside a −11 pp mean — is an estimator pathology on near-degenerate binary deltas (the large majority of paired deltas are zero), not evidence of a "stable low-variance confound."

**4. Re-operationalization as a Phase-2 forward design.** Embodiment heterogeneity (EH) and policy heterogeneity (PH) are re-operationalized through a tightly-coupled co-carry task (two manipulators rigidly carrying one shared object to a goal under tilt/internal-stress limits; design maintained in the project's planning records). **This is a Phase-2 / post-M10 forward design.** It adds nothing to the Phase-1 gate: this ADR's only Phase-1 footprint is recording a validation principle and reinterpreting an immutable archive, neither of which is Phase-1 gate work (invariant I1). No co-carry env, partner, or pre-registration is built under Phase 1 by this decision.

**5. The Stage-1 authorizing-evidence question.** With AS reinterpreted as construct-invalid, the Observation-Modality axis not fired, and the Stage-1 gate not moved, Stage 1 holds no axis that both cleared its gate and is coupling-valid. Resolved 2026-06-15 in the project decision record: **Stage 1 is accepted as rig-validation** (not a thesis-bearing cooperation result), the cooperation proof moves to the Phase-2 co-carry axis, and the M10 closure narrative is adjusted accordingly. Moving the Stage-1 gate into Phase 1 would be a Phase-1 scope change and is explicitly **not** adopted here.

## Rationale

The construct defect is legible from the environment source independent of the outcome's sign (the ego-only success predicate, the uncoupled scripted partner), so the criterion corrects a real flaw rather than rationalising an unwelcome result; the internal review verified this from code and reproduced the gate counts and the estimator pathology. Option A would record a construct-invalid number as thesis-bearing; Option B would re-spend credibility on a task that is construct-invalid even when cleanly measured; Option D leaves the immutable verdict uninterpreted while the defect is already established. Option C is chosen because it fixes the defect at the level that caused it and makes the heterogeneity claim testable through genuine cooperation — adopted only with the falsifiability test (Decision 2) and the explicit "this criterion is new" acknowledgement (Context), because without those it reads as an unfalsifiable escape hatch and as post-hoc instrument-attacking respectively.

## Consequences

- **Project scope (Phase 1):** none added. The Stage-1 gate, the staged plan, and the M10 calendar are unchanged by this ADR. The OM-unblock slice is not pursued as the next spend (decision 2026-06-15); the co-carry re-operationalization is Phase-2.
- **Project scope (Phase 2):** the co-carry env, the matched/policy-shift/embodiment-shift teammate set, the capability-calibration harness, and the PH/EH pre-registrations become Phase-2 work (design maintained in the project's planning records), sequenced and budgeted then; not committed here.
- **Supersession:** this ADR supersedes-in-part ADR-007 §Validation criteria + §Stage-1 framing — recorded in the ADR-INDEX (footnote h). The magnitude rule remains necessary but not sufficient; AS/OM promotion to Validated is **closed** under this ADR (non-coupling-valid as operationalized), not via the ≥20 pp gate. The "Stage-1 failure means the rig is broken, not the axis" framing of ADR-007 and this ADR's "the axis as operationalized is construct-invalid" are consistent: a non-coupling-valid task×axis is a rig/design property, and the corrective is re-operationalization (Phase 2), not an axis cut on the thesis. Optional one-line cross-notes in ADR-007/008/009/011 §Consequences are a minor follow-up docs touch; this ADR and the index are the authoritative record.
- **Reinterpretation, not editing (I8):** the `stage1-AS-2026-06-11` archive and all Phase-1 investigation directories remain immutable and unedited.
- **No schema bump (I9):** the future co-carry pre-registrations reuse the existing `chamber.evaluation.prereg` and SpikeRun schemas with new tags; this ADR triggers no `SCHEMA_VERSION` change. Any schema change discovered during the Phase-2 build is its own ADR.
- **Public surfaces:** the project README's Phase-status / leaderboard / ADR-count lines are corrected in a separate documentation touch (not part of this ADR).

## Risks and mitigations

- [risk] The criterion reads as narrative-rescue (post-result instrument-attacking). → Ground it in prior methodology (Evidence-basis reading note, citations predating the AS result); state in §Context that it is a new requirement; acknowledge the pre-registration-fidelity gap rather than folding it into the construct story.
- [risk] The criterion becomes an unfalsifiable escape hatch that dismisses every null as "not coupling-valid." → The Decision-2 positive-control + pre-committed null rule make coupling-validity measurable independent of the heterogeneity outcome.
- [risk] The co-carry redesign re-introduces a new confound (capability-gate selection bias truncating the hardest teammates; constraint-solver force mistaken for cooperative load; embodiment-shift conflating embodiment with controller style). → Pre-register the directional bias and archive excluded teammates; define the stress proxy as a constraint-solver force with a measured threshold; use the policy-shift measurements to bound the controller-style contribution. These bind the Phase-2 pre-statement, not Phase 1.
- [risk] The reinterpretation is read as "having it both ways" (keeping the immutable-verdict optics while discounting the result). → State plainly the verdict is uninterpretable in either direction; never cite the sign-reversal as "homogeneous is harder."

## Reversibility

**Type-1.** Adopting a validation principle that recharacterises two already-measured axes and reorients the heterogeneity test toward a new task is expensive to undo. The irreversible step is the recorded reinterpretation of the AS verdict. Exit ramp: this ADR may itself be superseded by a later ADR if a coupling-valid re-operationalization of AS/OM demonstrates the axes were salvageable; the immutable archives make any such re-litigation auditable.

## Validation criteria

This ADR is promoted to **Validated** when, in Phase 2: (1) a coupling-valid axis (co-carry PH or EH) is measured under a pre-registration that includes the coupling positive-control and the pre-committed null rule (Decision 2); (2) the positive control passes (single-robot success ≈ 0; tilt/stress constraints bind on successful matched episodes); and (3) the forward statistics hold — gate on the mean-delta with IQM reported as secondary, ≥10–12 seed-clusters before the bootstrap CI gates a decision, the capability-gate exclusion roster archived, and at-bar power stated honestly (n is not the minimum-detectable-effect lever). A qualifying heterogeneity drop earns the axis a benchmark slot; a qualifying null is reported as thesis-disconfirming.
